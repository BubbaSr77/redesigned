I grew up in a military family, which of course means that we moved round a lot! Growing up that way kinda forces you to be at least fairly resilient, cause otherwise you're just not gonna make it. Changing towns, schools, and friends got old after a while but we made it through but there was always some new adventure. When we PCSd from Alaska to Texas me my Dad and brother camped hiked and fished the whole way, zion canyon was AWESOME!!!



I joined the Florida Army National Guard in June of '95, and began going to drills through my senior year of highschool. I only stayed in the guard for a couple years before I got out, and began several other jobs just sortta floatin round. I had some many different jobs that first year after the guard from door to door sales (Kirby vacuums), to construction and then assistant store manager for a local convenience store. Then 9/11 happened, and like many others I joined the Army and served my country and my family for the next 14 years.


During my time in the Army I had acquired my share of injury and chronic pain problems to include a total knee replacement and pinched nerves. To say the least I was not progressing in the desired direction. As a result, I was sent through a MEB (medical evaluation board). Which is why I medically retired. I that exit process is where I found industrial robotics, I went through the training and worked that field for the next 6 years. Now I'm looking to add to my portfolio of skills that I can apply to the same industry. One of the best opportunities I've had was to teach basic robot programming to transitioning soldiers.
